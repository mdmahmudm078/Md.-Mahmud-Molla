Method notes

Classic Pearson square is most effective for balancing a target nutrient with two ingredients by cross-subtracting diagonally and converting the resulting parts to percentages.
For more than two ingredients, a common grouped method is to place high-protein ingredients in one group and low-protein ingredients in another, calculate the average CP of each group, apply Pearson square to the group averages, and then divide each group according to a chosen ratio.
